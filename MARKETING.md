# Product Marketing Context

_Last updated: 2026-03-25_

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** An AI agent that joins your video calls, transcribes everything, and turns meetings into searchable, actionable knowledge.
**What it does:** Vernix joins Zoom, Meet, Teams, and Webex calls as a participant. It transcribes in real time, generates summaries, extracts action items, and provides a live voice (or silent text) agent that answers questions using RAG across your meetings and uploaded documents.
**Product category:** Meeting intelligence / AI meeting assistant
**Product type:** SaaS (web app)
**Business model:** Freemium — Free (5 meetings), Pro ($29/mo), Unlimited ($99/mo)

## Target Audience

**Target companies:** Remote-first teams, engineering/product orgs, consultancies, agencies, leadership teams — any team where meetings are the primary decision-making channel.
**Decision-makers:** Team leads, engineering managers, product managers, founders, heads of ops.
**Primary use case:** Capturing, searching, and acting on what happens in video calls without manual note-taking.
**Jobs to be done:**

- "I need to remember what was decided in a meeting last week"
- "I need meeting notes without writing them myself"
- "I need to find something someone said across multiple calls"
- "I need action items tracked without manually creating tasks"

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Meeting knowledge is trapped — in people's heads, in forgotten recordings, in notes nobody reads. Decisions, context, and action items disappear the moment the call ends.
**Why alternatives fall short:**

- Otter.ai / Fireflies: transcription-only, no live Q&A, no cross-meeting RAG
- Manual notes: split attention, incomplete, biased toward the note-taker
- Recording playback: too slow to search, nobody rewatches
  **What it costs them:** Lost context, repeated discussions, missed action items, wasted time writing recaps.
  **Emotional tension:** Frustration of knowing something was said but not being able to find it. Guilt of not taking good enough notes. Anxiety about missing commitments.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** Otter.ai, Fireflies, Grain — transcription-focused, no live voice agent, limited cross-meeting search
**Secondary:** Manual note-taking, shared docs (Google Docs, Notion) — labor-intensive, incomplete
**Indirect:** Microsoft Copilot (Teams only), Google Recorder — platform-locked, not cross-platform

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**

- Live voice agent that answers questions during calls (unique)
- Silent mode for text-only participation
- Per-meeting vector collections for precise RAG retrieval
- Cross-meeting semantic search across all calls
- Knowledge base integration (upload your own docs)
- MCP integration for developer tools (Claude Desktop, Cursor)
- No plugins, no extensions — paste a link and go
  **Why customers choose us:** Voice Q&A during meetings is the hook. Cross-meeting search is the lock-in. Zero-friction setup is the conversion driver.

## Objections

| Objection                                               | Response                                                                                      |
| ------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Is it safe to have a bot on my call?"                  | Vernix doesn't store audio/video — only text transcripts. All data is scoped to your account. |
| "Do participants need to consent?"                      | Yes, and you should inform them. Vernix joins visibly as a named participant.                 |
| "What about accuracy?"                                  | Speaker-identified transcription powered by Recall.ai with AI-enhanced summarization.         |
| "What if I just want transcription, not a voice agent?" | Silent mode — bot joins quietly and responds via text chat only when mentioned.               |

**Anti-persona:** Teams that rarely have video calls. Companies with strict no-recording policies. Solo users who don't have meetings.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** Frustration with manual notes, missed action items, forgotten decisions, time wasted on recaps.
**Pull:** "Paste link and it works" simplicity, live Q&A, automatic summaries, searchable history.
**Habit:** Existing workflows (manual notes, Notion templates, "I'll remember it").
**Anxiety:** "Will it disrupt the meeting?", "Is my data safe?", "Will participants be annoyed?"
