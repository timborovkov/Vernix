Every sprint review follows the same script. Someone asks about a ticket, and the project manager opens Jira in another tab. Then someone references a design doc, so now Confluence is open too. Two tools, two tab switches, and the rest of the team is watching someone navigate instead of talking.

Connect Atlassian to Vernix, and Jira, Confluence, and Compass all live inside the meeting — through a single connection.

## What the Atlassian integration does

One OAuth authorization covers all three Atlassian products. During a call, ask the agent anything you'd normally look up manually:

- "What's the status of PROJ-123?"
- "What does our architecture doc say about the auth flow?"
- "Who's assigned to the remaining tickets in this sprint?"
- "Pull up the deployment runbook from Confluence"

The agent queries Jira, Confluence, or Compass depending on the question and responds in real time.

## Where this saves the most time

### Sprint reviews and planning

Sprint reviews exist to look at what got done and decide what's next. That means constantly referencing Jira. Instead of one person screen-sharing a board, anyone on the call can ask the agent for ticket status, blockers, or remaining scope. The data arrives on demand, so the conversation keeps moving.

### Architecture discussions

Technical discussions often reference documentation that lives in Confluence. "What did we decide about the caching strategy?" or "What does the API spec say about rate limits?" — these questions get answered immediately instead of someone promising to find the link after the call.

### Incident response

When something breaks, the first step is usually finding the relevant Jira ticket and any related runbooks. The agent can pull both while the team focuses on the actual problem. No one has to be the designated "Jira person" during the call.

## How to connect

Go to **Settings > Integrations** in your Vernix dashboard, find Atlassian, and click **Connect**. One OAuth authorization covers Jira, Confluence, and Compass — no separate connections for each product, no API keys to manage.

## Meetings that reference the work, not just discuss it

The gap between "what we talked about" and "what's actually in the system" is where decisions get lost. When Jira and Confluence data lives inside the meeting transcript, your notes capture not just the conversation but the state of the work at that moment.
